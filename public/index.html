  Because of the water shortage, each household in Montague was limited to 5,000 gallons of water a month. Families using more than this amount faced fines.

  The average household size in Montague in the 2010 census was 2.5. As it turns out, 5,000 gallons of water doesn't get the average family very far.

  If each family member takes a 10-minute shower every day, the average household will use 2,800 gallons of water in a month.

  If each person uses running water for 5 minutes every day, that's another 1,400 gallons per month.

  Flushing the toilet twice per day per person takes another 560 gallons of water.

  Just one load of dishes and one load of laundry per week will use 224 gallons of water.

  Even using water conservatively, the average household could easily use 5,000 gallons of water in a month. A family of three or more would almost surely face fines.
